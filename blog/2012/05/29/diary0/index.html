What I do

These days I’ve been tired with optimization of SQL. My DB instructor assigned a homework to insert 1 million random(but has some constrains and conditions) data into a database, and the speed determines our scores. I use Java to code a program with SQLite database, running on the computer with core-i3 3.4GHz and 4G memory. I tried my best and the time was limited into 3s to insert one million data. It may be fast enough for java, but I heard that someone can use less than 1 second to finish the job, with C and MySQL. What puzzled me is that the guy did not even use batch or tansaction to controll, but just the easiest way – insert 10k times, and 100 rows a time. Anyway, I’ve learnt the basic knowledge of DB that the less I/O, the faster you use. And this is why we close the auto_commit and add all queries to batch.
